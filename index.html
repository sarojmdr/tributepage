Sri Sri Newa Juju Jaya Bhupatindra Malla Deva

1674-1722

King of Bhaktapur

Bhupatindra Malla was a Malla Dynasty King of the Kingdom of Bhaktapur (present day Bhaktapur, Nepal) who reigned from 1696 until his death in 1722. He is the most widely known as king of Bhaktapur and is among the most popular of the Malla dynasty. He is popularly known in Bhaktapur as nepaḥ juju, meaning the king of the Newars. His reign was characterized by the construction of numerous palaces and temples with the Nyatapola temple being his most revered contribution. An integral part of the local folklore, Bhupatindra Malla is regarded as a great builder and a lover of arts whose reign is considered the cultural high point of Bhaktapur.

Contribution

  • Construction of the Nyatapola Temple
  • Treaty with the East India Company
  • Renovation of the 55-Window Palace
  • Works in the Taleju temple complex
  • Renovation of Nritya Vatsalā temple
  • Restoration of the Char Dham
